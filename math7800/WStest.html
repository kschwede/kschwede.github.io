Proof.

Proof.

Let ι:R→S be a finite extension of Noetherian rings with (R,𝔪) local, and assume ι splits as an R-module map. Thus there is an R-linear retraction π:S→R, so

S≅R⊕T

as R-modules.

F-injectivity. Since S is finite over R, for all i we have

H𝔪i⁢(S)≅H𝔪⁢Si⁢(S).

Local cohomology is additive, hence

H𝔪i⁢(S)≅H𝔪i⁢(R)⊕H𝔪i⁢(T).

Frobenius acts functorially on local cohomology and respects this splitting. If S is F-injective, then Frobenius is injective on H𝔪i⁢(S), hence also on the direct summand H𝔪i⁢(R). Therefore R is F-injective. ∎
